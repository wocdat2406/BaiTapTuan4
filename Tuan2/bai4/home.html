Getting Started

Scapbooking is the practice of combining photos, memorabilia, and stories in an album, preserving memories for future gennerations. In recent years, scapbooking has become a $300 milion dollar industry as the public has discovered the joys of creating albums for families and freinds. Preserving Your Memories

Scrapbook albums have existed since the beginning of photograpphy. However, the sad fact is that photograps and most printed material are not permanent: they will fade and yellow with age. Scapbookers of today are aware of these problems, and the industry is providing remedies to minimize deterioration. For the best result, avoid using material with high acid content which can cause photos and paper to deteriorate. Another thing to avoid is lignin, a material that is the bonding element in wood fibers. Over time, paper with lignin will become yellow and brittle, so you should only use lignin-free product.

Your albums should contain page protectors to shield the pages from smudges, oil, and dirt that can be tansferred from your hands. You should never albums with sticky "magnectic" pages. The sticky substance will be transferred to the photo and backing paper causing deterioration. Never crop ® Polariod photos: they will curl and fall apart. Mount all memorabilia on acid-free cardstock paper, and photocopy all newpaper clippings on acid-free paper.
